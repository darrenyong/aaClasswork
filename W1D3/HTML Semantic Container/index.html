Ingredients

For Soup

  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh grated ginger root
  • 4 garlic cloves, pressed or finely minced
  • 1 tablespoon chili garlic sauce
  • 2 tablenspoons osyter suace
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • ½ cup low sodium soy sauce
  • ¼ cup rice vinegar
  • 4 ounces shiitake mushrooms, stems removed and sliced
  • ½ - ¾ pounds baby bok choy, sliced into quarters lengthwise
  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 4 cups low sodium chicken broth
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 packages of fresh yakisoba noodles

For Egg

  • 4 eggs
  • ¼ cup low sodium soy sauce
  • ¼ cup rice vinegar
  • ¾ cup water

Additional Toppings (optional)

  • Sliced green onion
  • Cilantro
  • Chili garlic sauce
  • Sesame Seeds

Ramen

Description

Let's talk about ramen. And I’m not talking about the ten for a dollar packs beloved by college students the world around. I’m talking about the good stuff. A bowl of ramen is a total flavor bomb, filled with a rich, flavorful broth, tender meat, noodles, and topped with the crowning glory if you ask for it – the ramen egg. And there is a reason that ramen is so flavorful. It is pretty time-consuming to make a broth that complex.

Directions

To prepare the egg

  1. Whisk together the ¼ cup of soy sauce, ¼ cup of rice vinegar and ¾ cup water, set aside.
  2. Prepare an ice bath by filling a bowl with ice and water.
  3. Bring a pot of water to boil (make sure there is enough water to cover the eggs).
  4. When boiling, carefully lower the eggs into the water using a slotted spoon or spider. Reduce heat if necessary to keep the water just barely boiling.
  5. Cook for seven minutes, then remove from pot and place eggs in the ice bath.
  6. Leave in the ice bath for 3 minutes. Remove from ice bath and gently peel.
  7. Place peeled eggs in the marinade and make sure they are completely covered with the marinade.
  8. Leave in the marinade for several hours or overnight.

To prepare the noodles

  1. Bring a pot of water to a boil. When boiling, add the noodles and simmer for 3 minutes.
  2. Strain and rinse with cold water. Toss with just a little oil if necessary to keep them from sticking.

To prepare the soup

  1. Heat a large pot or dutch oven over medium high heat.
  2. Add the onion and saute until starting to soften, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the sliced mushrooms and saute for another 2-3 minutes.
  4. Add the garlic and ginger and stir fry for about 30 seconds, just until fragrant so that they don't burn.
  5. Add the chicken broth and 1 cup of water, soy sauce, rice vinegar, oyster sauce, fish sauce, chili garlic sauce, stir to combine.
  6. Bring back to a simmer.
  7. When broth reaches a simmer, add the chicken thighs.
  8. Simmer for 15-20 minutes until chicken is cooked through.

To serve the ramen

  1. In a bowl, place a serving of noodles, then pour the soup over them.
  2. Top soup with ramen egg, sliced green onions, cilantro and sesame seeds if desired.
